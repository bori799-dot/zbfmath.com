About

Mathematics with personality

I work at the intersection of mathematics, medicine, and computation. My research explores how mathematical models can help improve cancer treatments by predicting how tumours respond to therapies. I am particularly interested in how mathematical insight can support clinical decision-making and contribute to more personalised treatment strategies.

Research

How can mathematics help beat cancer?

Glioblastoma behaviour and treatment modelling

I develop mathematical models describing drug transport and fluid flow in glioblastoma. My work studies how interstitial flow, oedema, and cerebrovascular reactivity influence treatment effectiveness, and how electric fields used in electrochemotherapy can improve drug delivery to tumour tissue.

Patient-specific and clinically informed modelling

I work closely with clinicians to develop patient-specific models using MRI data and experimental observations. The aim is to build patient-specific tools that support understanding of treatment response and personalised therapy design.
